Explanation

Average mutual information measures the degree to which different stocks and market participants (FII, DII, retail) are behaving in increasingly similar ways. When this measure suddenly increases, it can indicate that the market is approaching a critical state or phase transition.

This synchronization often precedes major market movements, as it reflects a convergence of investor behavior that can amplify market dynamics.
